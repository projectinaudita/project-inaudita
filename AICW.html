Ethics Review — Executive Summary

The Aetherbridge Institute for Cognitive Welfare (AICW) conducts all research activities under strict adherence to international ethical standards, including the Declaration of Helsinki, the Belmont Report, and guidelines from authorized Institutional Review Boards (IRB). This review summarizes protocols related to participant welfare, data governance, and oversight mechanisms concerning the flagship research platform, The Locus.

Section 4 — Protocols & Safeguards

Participant recruitment is contingent upon documented informed consent, including thorough briefing on study objectives, potential risks, benefits, and voluntary withdrawal rights at any stage without penalty. Continuous monitoring for adverse events is maintained, with immediate response protocols and oversight by an independent safety committee. Regular internal and external audits ensure strict compliance with ethical mandates and participant safety.

Internal waiver COHORT-7 applies specific memory protocol exceptions authorized under governance clause 7.3. Details of these protocols are confidential and restricted.

Section 9 — Data Privacy & Memory Management

Participant data is anonymized, encrypted, and handled in full compliance with GDPR, HIPAA, and institutional data protection policies. Controlled recall procedures are conducted under strict audit trails, ensuring participant autonomy and data integrity. Data retention and archival follow institutional mandates and participant consent agreements.
