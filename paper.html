Executive Abstract

This dossier summarizes multi-modal pilot outcomes from the Resilience Optimization Protocols (ROP) v1.4, integrating neuroadaptive stimulus-conditioning, passive sentiment capture, and Bayesian hierarchical calibration of subject-level priors. Aggregate resilience indices (RI) improved nominally; however, emergent dissociative artifacts localize to Subject Cluster 07 and correlate with high-frequency Locus exposures. The document contains quantitative analyses, model specifications (GLM/Bayesian HLM), and restricted supplemental links for clearance-only materials.

1. Methods (Summary)

Participants (n=64; mean age 15.8 ± 1.3) were recruited across five district cohorts. Interventions deployed neuroadaptive modules with reinforcement schedules parameterized by Thompson sampling. Primary outcomes: Resilience Index (RI), Affective Congruence (AC), Compliance Drift (CD). Secondary telemetry: galvanic variance, eye-tracking dispersion, and passive sentiment capture (PSC) derived from ambient feed analysis.

1.1 Model Specification

We modelled RI using a hierarchical Bayesian GLM with logit link for bounded outcomes and random intercepts for cluster and subject-level slopes for exposure dosage:

\[\text{logit}(RI_{ij}) = \beta_0 + \beta_1 Dose_{ij} + \beta_2 AC_{ij} + u_j + \epsilon_{ij},\\ u_j \sim N(0,\sigma^2_u),\, \epsilon_{ij} \sim N(0,\sigma^2)\]

Prior distributions: weakly informative normals (\(\beta \sim N(0,1)\)), \(\sigma \sim \text{HalfCauchy}(0,1)\). Model fit via NUTS sampling (4 chains, 2000 warm-up, 3000 posterior draws).

1.2 Signal Processing & Feature Extraction

Audio & video streams underwent spectrotemporal decomposition (STFT), mel-frequency cepstral coefficient extraction (MFCC), and entropy-based anomaly detection (threshold = 2.9σ). PSC fused via late-stage ensemble stacking (XGBoost meta-learner) to predict compliance drift with AUROC benchmarks reported below.

2. Results

Primary outcome: posterior mean \(\Delta RI = +0.114\) (95% CI [0.062, 0.166]). Notable skew in residuals (Shapiro p < 0.01) suggested non-normality in Cluster 07. Predictive AUROC (PSC → CD) = 0.83.

2.2 Classification & Predictive Performance

PSC ensemble predicted Compliance Drift with AUROC = 0.83 (95% CI [0.79,0.87]). Feature importance: MFCC entropy (0.24), gaze dispersion (0.19), session engagement index (0.15), ambient sentiment (0.12), dosimetry (0.09).

3. Analysis

Residual diagnostics and posterior predictive checks indicate poor fit for Cluster 07 under the current generative assumptions — bimodality and heavy tails (kurtosis > 4). We explored mixture models (2-component Gaussian mixture) and an autoregressive exposure-lag model (AR(1) on session-level deltas). Mixture model posterior assigns 0.72 probability to anomalous component for Subject 07 cases.

3.1 The Locus Exposure Correlate

Textual and audio transcripts from The Locus sessions reveal thematic recurrence: memory displacement, echo narratives, and conditional compliance. Cross-modal embedding similarity (CLIP-derived vectors) shows increased alignment with negative valence templates post-exposure (cosine Δ = 0.18).

Figure 3. Heatmap — Cross-modal Valence Shift (post-pre)

Heatmap indicates cross-modal valence shift intensity; red indicates increased negative valence post Locus exposure.

3.2 Risk Stratification

Applying a Cox proportional hazards model on time-to-compliance-failure reveals hazard ratio HR=2.9 (Cluster 07 vs baseline, p<0.01). We recommend elevating monitoring thresholds and ceasing high-dosage Locus exposures pending root-cause analysis.

Conclusions & Recommendations

  1. Immediate hold on Phase 3 Locus-dosed modules for Cluster 07 individuals until diagnostics completed.
  2. Implement mixture-model informed screening in the real-time pipeline to flag high-anomaly posterior mass (>0.6).
  3. Commission independent auditory forensic analysis of Locus session assets (Appendix B).
